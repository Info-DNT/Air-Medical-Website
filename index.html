Frequently Asked Questions

The cost of an air ambulance depends on several factors, including the flight distance, the type of aircraft required, the patient's medical condition, and required airport/landing clearances. We provide fully detailed, transparent quotes in under 30 minutes with absolutely no hidden charges.

Emergency medical flights can often be mobilized within 2 to 4 hours after medical assessment and receiving clearances. For international evacuations, routing clearances, landing permits, and visa processing can take slightly longer, but our emergency team works 24X7 to secure approvals immediately.

Yes, for patients who are medically stable but require recumbent travel or medical supervision, we can arrange commercial airlines stretcher services or flight medical escorts. This is a highly cost-effective alternative to a private air ambulance charter.

Air Medical 24X7 provides global medical evacuation and repatriation services in over 193 countries, with primary operation hubs based in Dubai (U.A.E.) and New Delhi (India).

Our private air ambulances are equipped with state-of-the-art, FAA-approved intensive care unit (ICU) equipment, including ventilators, cardiac monitors, defibrillators, IV pumps, and dedicated emergency medications.

Yes, we manage the entire administrative process. Our global operations team coordinates directly with aviation authorities, customs, border control, and embassies to obtain all necessary visas, landing rights, and medical clearances.

Usually, yes. Depending on the aircraft size and the patient's clinical situation, 1 or 2 family members can accompany the patient on private air ambulances. On commercial flights, we can book additional adjacent seats for relatives.

An air ambulance is a private, custom-retrofitted ICU aircraft dedicated solely to one patient. A medical escort is a medical professional (doctor/nurse) who travels with a patient on a standard commercial airline flight to monitor their health and administer treatment.

Bed-to-bed transfer means we manage the entire transport journey: picking up the patient via ground ICU ambulance from their hospital bed, transferring them onto the aircraft, and taking them via ground ambulance to the destination hospital room.

Every mission is staffed by highly trained medical flight crews, including intensive care specialists, pediatricians, cardiologists, or ICU nurses, depending on the patient's diagnosis and medical needs.
