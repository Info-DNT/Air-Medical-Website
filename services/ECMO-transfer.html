What Is ECMO Transfer?

ECMO is a highly specialized form of life support for patients who suffer from severe respiratory failure (acute respiratory distress syndrome) or severe cardiac failure (cardiac arrest), when all other methods of treating their conditions are no longer sufficient.

During ECMO therapy, blood is drained from the patient's body into an external circuit. In this circuit, the blood is oxygenated and carbon dioxide is removed before the processed blood is returned to the patient's body, allowing both the heart and lungs time to recuperate.

Transferring an ECMO patient is one of the most difficult and dangerous types of medical transfer available. To perform an ECMO transfer successfully, specialized aircraft must be used along with particular types of ECMO medical equipment, as well as a highly trained team of multidisciplinary personnel.

At Air Medical 24X7, we provide continuous ECMO support from the original point of care to the final destination, ensuring the stability of the patient throughout the entire transport process.
